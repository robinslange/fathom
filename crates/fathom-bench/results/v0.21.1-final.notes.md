## Choice: RRF k=30

Three configurations tie on iconic-top-10 count (the load-bearing criterion): rrf-k30, linear-a0.5, linear-a0.6. Tiebreakers:

- **rrf-k30** wins on adversarial noise floor (0.032 — order of magnitude below linear's 0.5-0.6).
- RRF is parameter-free; linear depends on a tuned α that may need re-calibration as the corpus or embedder evolves.
- rrf-k60 has higher MRR (0.589 vs 0.533) but loses 1 iconic top-10 hit.

Recommended default: **`FATHOM_FUSION_MODE=rrf`, `FATHOM_RRF_K=30`**.

This is a CHANGE from the spec's k=10 default. Reason: the spec's reasoning ("low k amplifies BM25 rank-1 signal where dense fails") assumed the dense lane was weak. In practice, since v0.20 the dense lane has improved markedly (bge-small re-embedding pipeline) and dense-only already hits recall@50=94.1%. With both lanes strong, lower k introduces noise from BM25's lexical outliers rather than rescuing genuinely-missing chunks. k=30 gives BM25 enough weight to rescue the iconic-phrase case while damping its noise on topical queries.

## What surfaces top-10 under rrf-k30 that doesn't under dense-only

- "appearances are not realities and what is is not what seems" — was a hard miss in dense; now still misses top-50 but at least rrf gives BM25 a stronger pull. (Curated alias dict path is the actual rescue here; this is one of the four alias-guarded queries.)
- Numerically, rrf-k30 has 13/15 vs dense's 12/16 in top-10. The difference is that fused modes **lose** the "what is past is done with" query (rank 42 in dense → not in top-50 in fused) because Aristotle's *Nicomachean Ethics* contains the verbatim phrase "what is done and past" which BM25 ranks higher than Marcus's Meditations. This is a real lexical-vs-semantic tradeoff; no fusion mode in this sweep fixes it.

## What dense-only does that fused doesn't

Dense-only has higher recall@50 (94.1% vs 88.2%) — fusion is *removing* some genuinely-relevant chunks from top-50 that pure dense includes. The 6pp drop is the cost of letting BM25 vote. Acceptable given the gains in hits@10 and the much lower adversarial floor.

## Caveat — iconic-guard

The iconic-phrase alias dictionary fires unconditionally (before fusion-mode dispatch). The 4 alias-guarded queries (Apology, Descartes, "only thing I know", Plato dialogues) score similarity=1.0 in every mode. They inflate "iconic top-10" counts uniformly. The dense-only iconic top-10 = 12/16 includes them; rrf-k30 iconic top-10 = 13/15 includes them. To re-state without alias-guard inflation: dense alone gets 8/12 non-alias iconic queries in top-10; rrf-k30 gets 9/11.

## Comparison vs v0.20 baseline

All v0.21.1 fused modes meet or exceed v0.20 on every metric except recall@50 for fused modes (88.2% vs 76.5% — still a 12pp gain). Dense-only beats v0.20 by 18pp on recall.
